Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about salary, income, taxes, and career.

Annual salary = Hourly rate × Hours per week × Weeks per year. For a standard full-time job: Hourly rate × 40 hours × 52 weeks = Annual salary. Example: $25/hr × 40 × 52 = $52,000/year.
Gross salary is your total pay before any deductions. Net salary (take-home pay) is what you actually receive after income tax, social security, pension contributions, and other deductions are subtracted. Net salary is typically 65–80% of gross salary depending on your country and tax bracket.
Overtime pay in the US is typically 1.5× your regular hourly rate for hours worked beyond 40 per week. Some employers pay 2× (double time) for holidays or excessive hours. Formula: Overtime pay = Regular rate × 1.5 × Overtime hours. Example: $20/hr regular × 1.5 = $30/hr overtime.
To calculate your minimum freelance rate: 1) Determine your target annual income. 2) Add annual business expenses. 3) Calculate billable hours (hours per week × working weeks, typically 48). 4) Divide total needed by billable hours. 5) Add your desired profit margin. Use our Freelance Rate Calculator for an instant result.
A standard full-time employee works 2,080 hours per year (40 hours/week × 52 weeks). After accounting for typical paid time off (10 vacation days + 10 holidays = 20 days = 160 hours), actual productive hours are closer to 1,920/year. Freelancers typically have 1,440–1,920 billable hours after non-billable work time.
$25/hour equals approximately $52,000/year (full-time). This is above the US median individual income (~$40,000) and can be a comfortable salary in lower cost-of-living areas. In expensive cities like New York or San Francisco, $25/hour may be tight. The 'good salary' threshold depends entirely on your location, lifestyle, and financial goals.
To convert annual salary to hourly: Divide annual salary by 2,080 (standard working hours). Example: $60,000 ÷ 2,080 = $28.85/hour. Or use the quick formula: Annual salary ÷ 52 weeks ÷ 40 hours = Hourly rate. Our Annual to Monthly Calculator does this instantly.
Employee benefits typically add 25–40% to your base salary in total compensation value. Key benefits include: Health insurance ($6,000–$20,000/year), 401k match (3–6% of salary), paid time off ($3,000–$8,000/year), life insurance, and professional development. Always factor benefits into job offer comparisons.
