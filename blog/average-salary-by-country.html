Why salaries differ so much between countries

  • Cost of living: Higher cost countries need higher nominal wages to maintain comparable purchasing power
  • Productivity: Countries with higher worker productivity can afford higher wages
  • Labour market supply and demand: Skill scarcity drives up wages
  • Minimum wage laws: Countries with strong minimum wage legislation have higher wage floors
  • Union strength: Strong unions negotiate higher wages and better benefits

Purchasing power vs nominal salary

A $65,000 salary in the US has different real purchasing power than the equivalent in Switzerland ($85,000). Always compare cost of living alongside nominal salaries when evaluating opportunities in different countries.
